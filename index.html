about me.


Hello there, my name is Luis Cermeno. Thank you for visiting my webpage! I'm a junior student majoring in EECS (Electrical Engineering and Computer Science) in the University of California, Berkeley.

More than often I get asked, don't you get bored coding? To answer that I always point out that, deep inside every human, is the need to create. Some, express this by engineering infrastucture and/or machines. Other professionals, research and create knowledge. When programming, developers lose the sense of time because building an application is one of the purest forms of creativity. You are literally seeing how your product builds from scratch. You can see how every move you make affects the outcome instantly. Particularly, I have a thing for creating a comfortable user experience through beautiful and responsive graphic interfaces. Software development allows me to express myself and my passion.

Moreover, the huge community of developers and the rapid advancement of information processing devices makes it virtually impossible for a single developer to know every technology available. This makes making software even more interesting. Being able to constantly learn from you colleages is one of my favorite parts.

A little bit more about me. I love playing fingerstyle guitar. A good way to free up my mind when I get stuck when coding is to play. There's definetly some wires in our brains connected that must relate our logical and artistic side. But, lets leave that to our genious neuroscientists. When I'm not building software, I play tennis. I am a decent 3.5 NTRP. It is not pro by far, but I hope that someday I can get there! I also love roadtripping, hiking and photography (hence every picture in the website , find more in gallery). The best combo!. Some of my most precious memories happened on trips. Every time I look at the beauty of the ocean in Point Reyes (picture below), I feel closer to my best friends.
